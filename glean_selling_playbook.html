The 6 objections you'll hear most, and how to handle them.

Cost
"It's too expensive."
Reframe: "If each employee saves 30 minutes a day on search, what's that worth at your average fully-loaded salary? For a 300-person company that's typically $2–4M/year recovered. Glean costs a fraction of that." Then offer a department pilot to prove ROI before full commitment.
Competition
"We're already using M365 Copilot."
Reframe: "Copilot is powerful — but it only sees Microsoft data. If you're also on Salesforce, Jira, Slack, or Zendesk, Glean fills the gaps Copilot can't touch. They're complementary, not competitors."
Timing
"We're not ready for this yet."
Reframe: "Your competitors are already deploying this. The longer knowledge fragmentation continues, the more ramp time you lose on every new hire." Offer a no-pressure pilot scoped to one team, 30 days.
IT resistance
"IT won't approve another tool."
Reframe: "Glean doesn't copy or store your data in a new silo — it indexes it in place, respects your existing permissions, and is SOC 2 compliant. It's built to pass enterprise security review." Offer to do a security review session directly with their IT team.
Adoption
"People won't use it."
Reframe: "Glean puts itself inside Slack, Chrome, and the tools people already use — it doesn't require a behavior change. Usage typically starts day one." Reference the '3 weeks to go live' case study from Glean's own customers.
DIY
"We can build this ourselves."
Reframe: "100+ native connectors, permission-aware indexing, and enterprise security are 18 months of engineering if you build it. Your team could be live in 3 weeks with Glean." Ask: what's the opportunity cost of those engineering months?
